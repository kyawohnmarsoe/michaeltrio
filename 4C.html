Colour

Colour

Diamond Colour : M

Colour refers to the natural colour of the diamond visible to the naked eye and will not change over time. Following the GIA diamond colour-grading system, the degree of colour ranges from D (colourless) to Z (faint or light yellow).

M
Faint colour visible to the naked eye. However, even with colour presence, these diamonds can still emit fire and beauty. At Michael Trio, we do not offer L and M colour diamonds.
L
Faint colour visible to the naked eye. However, even with colour presence, these diamonds can still emit fire and beauty. At Michael Trio, we do not offer L and M colour diamonds.
K
Faint colour visible to the naked eye. However, even with colour presence, these diamonds can still emit fire and beauty. At Michael Trio, we do not offer L and M colour diamonds.
J
Has a slightly detectable colour. Both grades also typically appear colourless to the untrained eye and are of excellent value.
I
Has a slightly detectable colour. Both grades also typically appear colourless to the untrained eye and are of excellent value.
H
Noticeable colour only when compared with higher colour-graded diamonds. Otherwise, both grades appear colourless to the untrained eye and are of excellent value.
G
Noticeable colour only when compared with higher colour-graded diamonds. Otherwise, both grades appear colourless to the untrained eye and are of excellent value.
F
Colourless with minute traces of colour that can only be detected by a trained gemologist.
E
Colourless with only minute traces of colour. E-graded diamonds are also rare and emit high levels of brilliance.
D
Absolutely colourless and the highest quality colour-grade available. D-graded diamonds are extremely rare and emit unrivaled brilliance.

Clarity

Clarity

Diamond Clarity : FL

Clarity is the assessment of imperfections known as blemishes on the surface and inclusions within a diamond. The clarity of a diamond can be determined by the type, number, and size of these inclusions when viewed under 10x magnifi- cation.

FL
No inclusions and blemishes visible under 10x magnification
IF
Small surface blemishes with no inclusions visible under 10x magnification
VVS1 & VVS2
Miniscule inclusions that are difficult for trained eyes to see under 10x magnification
VS1 & VS2
Minor inclusions observed with effort under 10x magnification
SI1 & SI2
Inclusions noticeable under 10x magnification
I1 & I2 & I3
Inclusions are obvious under 10x magnification, brilliance and beauty likely affected. At Michael Trio, we do not offer I1, I2, and I3 diamonds

Cut

Cut

Diamond Cut : Good

Cut refers to the ability of a diamond to refract light, produce brilliance, and fire up through to the table and crown. Precise workmanship will be required to ensure that a diamond is trimmed to maximise its brilliance and fire. The cut is a crucial component when determining the beauty of a diamond. A well-cut diamond will appear more brilliant and larger than other diamonds of a similar carat weight.

Good
A premium cut that maximises value without compromising on quality.
Very Good
A high-quality cut that reflects almost as much light as an Ideal cut, while at a substantially lower cost
Ideal
An exceptional quality cut that produces the optimal balance of brilliance and fire as it reflects almost all light that enters the diamond. Only 3% of the worlds diamonds are Ideal cut.
Pure Hearts TM
The finest cut that is unmatched in pristine brilliance, fire, and has precise optical symmetry.
